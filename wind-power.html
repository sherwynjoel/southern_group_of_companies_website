Company Overview

The Group's Flagship
Clean Energy Venture

Southern Wind Power India Private Limited is the renewable energy flagship of the Southern Group, incorporated in Tamil Nadu on 26 May 2022. The company holds a broad mandate to generate power from all sources of energy and to conduct dedicated R&D on wind and solar technologies.

Southern Wind Power is positioned as the primary growth vehicle for the Group's clean energy ambitions — focusing on wind and solar power generation, dedicated energy R&D, and energy auditing services.

Market Context

India's Renewable Energy
Opportunity

India is the world's third-largest renewable energy market and growing faster than any major economy. Wind and solar together are the backbone of India's 500 GW target by 2030 — and Tamil Nadu is at the centre of this national transition.

250 GW

Non-Fossil Fuel Capacity

India crossed 250 GW of non-fossil fuel installed capacity in September 2025 — a 3× increase since 2014. India now ranks 3rd globally in renewable energy and achieved a 50% non-fossil power mix 5 years ahead of schedule.

Source: MNRE / PIB, 2025
150 GW

Solar Installed Capacity

India's cumulative solar capacity reached 150.26 GW as of 31 March 2026 — including 110.43 GW utility-scale and 25.73 GW rooftop. India deployed a record 44.6 GW of solar in FY 2025–26 alone, exceeding its own 34 GW target.

Source: MNRE, March 2026
12 GW

Tamil Nadu Wind Capacity

Tamil Nadu holds 11,938 MW of installed wind capacity — second in India with a 22% national share. The state's capacity utilisation factor is expected to reach 29.6% in 2025–26, the highest since 2018–19.

Source: NITI Aayog ICED / MNRE, 2025
19.05%

Solar Market CAGR 2026–31

India's solar energy market grows at 19.05% CAGR — from 145 GW in 2026 to 348 GW by 2031. Wind energy is equally strong at 14.97% CAGR, rising from 53 GW to 119 GW over the same period.

Source: Mordor Intelligence, 2025

Tamil Nadu's wind corridor — spanning from the Palghat Gap through the Western Ghats to Kanyakumari — is one of Asia's richest wind energy zones. With 11,938 MW of installed capacity, a growing fleet of hybrid wind-solar projects, and the highest capacity utilisation factor in nearly a decade, Tamil Nadu offers unparalleled conditions for wind power development and applied R&D.

India's solar momentum is unprecedented — deploying 37.9 GW in 2025 alone and adding rooftop capacity at a 161% year-on-year rate. Solar capacity has surged 42× since 2014 (from 2.82 GW to 123 GW). PM Surya Ghar targets 1 crore households, while PM-KUSUM adds 34.8 GW of agricultural solar — creating vast demand for solar generation, installation, and O&M services.

Policy & Regulatory Tailwinds

500 GW

National RE Target by 2030

India has already crossed 250 GW and must add another 250 GW by 2030 — requiring 50+ GW of annual additions. Tamil Nadu is among the top states earmarked for new wind and solar capacity allocation.

MNRE

39% Budget Increase

The MNRE budget for FY 2025–26 rose 39% to ₹256.49 crore. In 2024, 83% of India's total power sector investment went to clean energy — signalling a decade-long policy commitment to renewable deployment.

2070

Net-Zero Commitment

India's net-zero by 2070 pledge and its 50% renewable power target — already achieved in June 2025, five years ahead of schedule — create a sustained multi-decade tailwind for wind, solar, R&D, and energy services.
