Company Overview

The Group's Newest
Solar Energy Venture

Southern Solar Co-op India Ltd is the most recently incorporated company in the Southern Group, registered in Tamil Nadu on 26 February 2026. The company brings a dedicated focus to solar power — spanning the full project lifecycle from development and EPC to operations, maintenance, and carbon market trading.

With three founding promoters and a mandate that covers solar plants, parks, and rooftop systems, Southern Solar Co-op is positioned to capitalize on India's accelerating solar energy transition.

Market Context

India's Solar EPC &
O&M Opportunity

India is the world's fastest-growing solar market — adding a record 44.6 GW in a single financial year and targeting 500 GW of clean energy by 2030. Tamil Nadu sits at the epicentre of this transition, with vast untapped rooftop potential and a thriving utility-scale pipeline.

150 GW

India's Solar Installed Base

India's cumulative installed solar capacity crossed 150 GW as of 31 March 2026 — including 110 GW utility-scale, 25.7 GW rooftop, and 14.1 GW KUSUM off-grid projects. India now ranks 3rd globally in solar capacity.

Source: MNRE, March 2026
44.6 GW

Record Annual Solar Addition

India added a record 44.6 GW of solar in FY 2025–26, exceeding its own 34 GW target by 31%. Every gigawatt installed requires EPC execution and long-term O&M contracts — creating direct business for solar service companies.

Source: MNRE / PIB, 2026
10.1%

India Solar EPC CAGR

India's solar EPC sector is growing at 10.1% CAGR — outpacing the global average of 8.1%. The India ground-mounted solar EPC market is projected to grow at 12–16% CAGR through 2035, reaching 40–55 GW in annual installations.

Source: GM Insights / IndexBox, 2025
161%

Rooftop Solar YoY Growth

India's rooftop solar additions grew 161% year-on-year in the first nine months of 2025, driven by PM Surya Ghar. Tamil Nadu's rooftop penetration stands at just 1.55% — less than 2% of potential utilized — signalling massive headroom for EPC companies.

Source: Mercom India / Blue Bright Energy, 2025

Tamil Nadu's rooftop solar penetration stands at just 1.55% of annual electricity demand — less than 2% of potential utilized — making it one of India's largest untapped solar installation markets. Driven by PM Surya Ghar subsidies (₹78,000 for 3+ kW systems), TNGECL's 40 MW rooftop tender for government buildings, and rising electricity tariffs, Tamil Nadu's rooftop solar market is poised for rapid acceleration through 2030.

Policy & Regulatory Tailwinds

Surya Ghar

PM Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli

₹75,021 crore outlay targeting rooftop solar in 1 crore Indian households. As of December 2025, 23.9 lakh homes have already installed 7 GW — creating a sustained, subsidy-backed demand pipeline for rooftop EPC companies.

KUSUM

PM-KUSUM Agricultural Solar

PM-KUSUM targets 34.8 GW of agricultural solar with ₹34,422 crore in central financial support — covering solar pumps, feeder-level solarisation, and ground-mounted plants, all requiring dedicated EPC and O&M contracts.

500 GW

National Clean Energy Target

India's 500 GW non-fossil capacity target by 2030 requires adding 50+ GW annually through 2030. Solar is the primary vehicle — and every installed megawatt creates a 25-year O&M opportunity for service companies like Southern Solar Co-op.
